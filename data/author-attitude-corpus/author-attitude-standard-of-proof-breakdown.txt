Standard of Proof Rules:
- Direct tone proof: the stem directly asks for the author's attitude or best description without signaling an extra inference jump.
- Supported inference: the stem uses language like based on the passage, suggests, implies, can be inferred, or would describe, so the answer still needs close textual support but allows a modest inference.
- Phrase-level proof: the stem asks which phrase or local wording reveals the attitude, so the proof should come from a specific expression rather than the whole passage tone alone.
