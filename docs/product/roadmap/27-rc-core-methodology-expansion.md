# RC Core Methodology Expansion

**Purpose:** Define the roadmap bet for expanding the core Reading Comprehension methodology modules.
**Audience:** Maintainers, content editors, and future agents planning course improvements.
**Status:** active
**Source of truth:** yes
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-17
**Related docs:** [README.md](./README.md), [vision-and-decision-filter.md](./vision-and-decision-filter.md), [../../product/course-content-map.md](../../product/course-content-map.md)

## User Problem

The Reading Comprehension (RC) methodology modules (Modules 23-40) are structurally thin, typically containing only 2-4 lessons per module. In contrast, Logical Reasoning (LR) modules often contain 10-14 lessons. This imbalance leaves students with insufficient guided practice and step-by-step breakdown of specific RC question types before they face full practice passages. The course map looks comprehensive, but the actual depth in RC is lacking.

## Proposed Direction

Expand the core RC methodology modules to achieve parity with the depth of LR modules. This involves adding more granular "Step-by-Step" guide lessons, lessons on identifying common trap answers specific to each question type (e.g., "RC: Main Idea"), and more focused drills that isolate specific skills before moving to full passages.

## Why Now

The current course framework is stable and content delivery mechanisms are proven. However, the contrast between LR depth and RC shallowness is the most glaring content gap in the current curriculum map. Addressing this now will provide a more balanced and complete LSAT preparation experience, directly impacting student confidence in the RC section.

## Dependencies

- Requires sourcing high-quality, real LSAT passage excerpts for drills from the `module49` and `module53` question banks.
- May require updates to validation scripts if new lesson structures or metadata types are introduced.
- Depends on clear adherence to the content policy (real material for Lesson 4+).

## Risks

- Significant content creation effort.
- Risk of bloating the curriculum if the new lessons are not highly focused and effective.
- Potential disruption to existing student progress markers if module structures change dramatically.

## Likely Upside

- Substantially stronger Reading Comprehension product offering.
- Better student outcomes and higher satisfaction due to more comprehensive methodology instruction.
- Creates a more symmetric and internally coherent curriculum map.

## First Practical Milestone

Draft and sequence a comprehensive 10-lesson plan for the "RC: Inference" module (Module 24) as a pilot for the expansion methodology, utilizing real LSAT material from the question banks.
