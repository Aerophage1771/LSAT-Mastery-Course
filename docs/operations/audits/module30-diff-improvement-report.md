# Module 30: Author Viewpoint -- Website Content Improvement Report

**Scope:** The 2 website lessons (30-1 through 30-2) evaluated as a standalone course unit.
**Date:** 2026-04-01

---

## Executive Summary

Module 30 covers Author Viewpoint questions in two lessons: Introduction (30-1) and Step-by-Step Approach (30-2). Author Viewpoint is one of the highest-frequency RC question types (~12% of all RC questions, 252 appearances), making this module critically important. The content is solid, with a strong emphasis on textual proof. Key improvement opportunities:

1. **No practice questions.** Neither lesson includes a question-card or worked example. For a ~12% frequency question type, this is a significant gap.
2. **Overlap with Module 29 (Author Attitude) is not addressed.** The distinction between Viewpoint (what the author believes) and Attitude (how the author feels) needs explicit treatment.
3. **Lesson 30-2's "Treat Each Answer as a Hypothesis" approach is the module's best teaching tool** and deserves a concrete demonstration.
4. **The "Check Ownership" point in 30-2 is critical** -- confusing the author's view with a third party's view is a common error that deserves more emphasis (and connects to Module 32).

---

## Lesson-by-Lesson Review

### Lesson 30-1: Introduction (11 blocks)

**Role:** Module introduction, stem identification, and importance statement.

**Strengths:**
- Clear four-clue detection framework (central argument, tone and degree, logical implications, evaluative language) for identifying the author's viewpoint.
- Four stem categories (Direct Agreement, Disagreement/Negative, Comparative Viewpoint, Judgment/Valuation) provide comprehensive coverage.
- The Disagreement category (LEAST likely to believe, would disagree with) is valuable -- many students mishandle negative viewpoint stems.
- Frequency data (252 times, ~12%) correctly positions this as a top-priority question type.
- The importance section makes the correct connection to Main Conclusion and passage comprehension.

**Issues:**
- **No distinction from Author Attitude (Module 29).** The opening mentions "opinions" and "tone" which overlaps with Attitude territory. A clear callout would help.
- **Redundancy.** The opening paragraph and the Overarching Takeaway both describe the goal as finding what the author would endorse.
- **The Judgment/Valuation stem category overlaps with Attitude stems.** "Values most highly" and "attitude toward" could appear in either module. A note about overlap would help.
- **No practice question.**

**Suggested edits:**
- Add a callout: "Viewpoint vs. Attitude: Viewpoint asks WHAT the author believes. Attitude asks HOW the author feels about a topic."
- Note the stem overlap between Viewpoint and Attitude and explain how to handle it.
- Add a mini-exercise.

---

### Lesson 30-2: Step-by-Step Approach (10 blocks)

**Role:** The 2-step method plus two tie-breaker strategies.

**Strengths:**
- Step 1 (Synthesize the Author's Core Argument and Tone) asks three excellent diagnostic questions: What is their claim? What is their attitude? What are their reasons?
- Step 2's "Treat Each Answer Choice as a Hypothesis to Be Proven" is a strong analytical framework.
- The "Find Direct Proof" / "Eliminate Unsupported Claims" / "Check Ownership" sub-instructions are precise and actionable.
- The "Check Ownership" point is critical -- it warns against selecting statements that belong to a third party the author is criticizing.
- Tie-Breaker #1 (The Scope Test) addresses the narrow-vs-broad answer distinction.
- Tie-Breaker #2 (The Intensity Check) teaches calibration of answer certainty to passage language.

**Issues:**
- **No worked example.** The hypothesis-testing method is described but never demonstrated.
- **No question-card.**
- **The "Check Ownership" point deserves more emphasis.** It's currently a bullet in a list but represents one of the most common errors on viewpoint questions. A callout or separate section would be appropriate.

**Suggested edits:**
- Add a worked example demonstrating the hypothesis-testing approach on 2-3 answer choices.
- Promote "Check Ownership" to a standalone callout or step.
- Add a question-card.

## Structural Issues

### 1. No Practice Questions -- High-Frequency Type

Author Viewpoint at ~12% frequency is the second-most common RC question type (after Inference). The complete absence of practice is a critical gap.

**Recommendation:** Add at least one question-card to Lesson 30-2, ideally two.

### 2. No Viewpoint/Attitude Distinction

Modules 29 and 30 cover closely related skills. The overlap in stems (e.g., "values most highly") and concepts (tone, opinion) means students will confuse them without explicit guidance.

**Recommendation:** Add reciprocal callouts in both 29-1 and 30-1 distinguishing the two types.

### 3. Ownership Check Deserves Elevation

The "Check Ownership" point in 30-2 addresses the same skill as Module 32 (Third-Party Viewpoint). A cross-reference would help students connect these modules.

**Recommendation:** Elevate "Check Ownership" to a standalone callout and add a forward reference to Module 32.
