# Module 27: Application -- Website Content Improvement Report

**Scope:** The 2 website lessons (27-1 through 27-2) evaluated as a standalone course unit.
**Date:** 2026-04-01

---

## Executive Summary

Module 27 covers Application questions in two lessons: Introduction (27-1) and Step-by-Step Approach (27-2). The module is structurally parallel to Module 26 (Analogy) and shares many of its strengths and gaps. The checklist approach in 27-2 is the module's standout teaching tool. Key improvement opportunities:

1. **No practice questions.** Neither lesson includes a question-card or full worked example.
2. **Strong overlap with Module 26 (Analogy).** Both modules teach "abstract a principle, then match to answer choices." The key distinction -- Analogy asks for a parallel situation while Application asks for correct use of a rule -- is stated but could be more sharply drawn.
3. **The tie-breaker strategies in 27-2 are nearly identical to 26-2.** "Focus on the Difference" appears in both modules verbatim. This could be a deliberate design choice (consistent framework) or unintentional redundancy.
4. **Missing: common trap answer patterns.** No distractor lesson.

---

## Lesson-by-Lesson Review

### Lesson 27-1: Introduction (10 blocks)

**Role:** Module introduction, stem identification, and importance statement.

**Strengths:**
- Clear opening definition that distinguishes Application from Analogy: "take a rule, standard, or theory... and correctly apply it to a brand new situation."
- The three-step framework (Isolate the principle, Understand conditions, Find the matching scenario) mirrors the Analogy module's approach, which creates helpful consistency.
- Two stem categories (Applying a Principle, Predicting Hypothetical Results) accurately cover the question landscape.
- The Overarching Takeaway correctly emphasizes applying the passage's logic, not your own.
- Frequency data (~73 times, ~3.5%) and the note about testing "deep comprehension" provide useful framing.

**Issues:**
- **Redundancy between opening and stem identification intro.** The stem section's intro paragraph ("Application questions ask you to take a rule, principle, theory...") restates the opening almost verbatim.
- **No practice question.** No worked example or exercise.
- **The distinction between Application and Analogy could be sharper.** Both ask students to match passage logic to new scenarios. A brief "How This Differs From Analogy" callout would prevent confusion.

**Suggested edits:**
- Cut the redundant intro paragraph from the stem identification section.
- Add a callout distinguishing Application from Analogy.
- Add a mini-exercise.

---

### Lesson 27-2: Step-by-Step Approach (10 blocks)

**Role:** The 3-step method plus two tie-breaker strategies.

**Strengths:**
- The checklist approach in Step 2 (Isolate the Principle and Its Conditions) is the module's best teaching tool. The legal fraud example (intentional? material? misrepresentation?) is concrete and memorable.
- Step 3's instruction to "treat each answer choice as a separate case study" is clear and actionable.
- Tie-Breaker #1 (Strict Re-Check) correctly identifies that the error is usually in misreading the principle, not the scenarios.

**Issues:**
- **Tie-Breaker #2 ("Focus on the Difference") is identical to Module 26.** The text is nearly word-for-word the same. This could be intentional for consistency, but a module-specific variation would be more helpful.
- **No worked example demonstrating the checklist approach.** The legal fraud checklist is shown in the abstract but never applied to an actual answer choice.
- **No question-card.**

**Suggested edits:**
- Add a worked example showing the checklist applied to 2-3 answer choices (one correct, one failing a condition).
- Customize Tie-Breaker #2 with an Application-specific example.
- Add a question-card.

---

## Structural Issues

### 1. No Practice Questions

Same gap as Modules 24-26. The checklist approach is inherently hands-on and would benefit enormously from a worked example.

**Recommendation:** Add at least one question-card to Lesson 27-2 demonstrating the checklist method.

### 2. Overlap with Module 26

The teaching structure (abstract principle + match to choices) is shared between Application and Analogy. The key distinction (Analogy = parallel structure, Application = correct use of a rule) should be made explicit early in 27-1.

**Recommendation:** Add a "How This Differs From Analogy" callout to Lesson 27-1.

### 3. No Trap/Distractor Lesson

Common Application traps include: scenarios that meet most conditions but fail one, scenarios that meet all conditions but for a different principle, and scenarios that confuse necessary with sufficient conditions.

**Recommendation:** Add a brief traps section to Lesson 27-2.
