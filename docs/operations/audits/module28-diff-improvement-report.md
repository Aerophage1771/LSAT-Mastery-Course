# Module 28: Approach -- Website Content Improvement Report

**Scope:** The 2 website lessons (28-1 through 28-2) evaluated as a standalone course unit.
**Date:** 2026-04-01

---

## Executive Summary

Module 28 covers Approach questions (identifying the author's argumentative strategy or passage structure) in two lessons: Introduction (28-1) and Step-by-Step Approach (28-2). This is the rarest RC question type (~0.5% of questions, only 11 appearances), and the module appropriately frames it as a transferable skill rather than a high-frequency target. Key improvement opportunities:

1. **No practice questions.** Neither lesson includes a question-card or worked example.
2. **The "Passage Blueprint" method in 28-2 is excellent** and deserves a concrete demonstration.
3. **Lesson 28-1 leans heavily on comparative-passage stems.** Two of three stem categories (Similarity, Difference) are comparative-passage-specific. A single-passage Approach question category should receive equal weight.
4. **The tie-breaker strategies are the module's strongest tactical content** -- "Focus on the Verbs" and "Check the Scope and Emphasis" are precise and actionable.

---

## Lesson-by-Lesson Review

### Lesson 28-1: Introduction (10 blocks)

**Role:** Module introduction, stem identification, and importance statement.

**Strengths:**
- Clear opening with a concrete "blueprint" metaphor for what Approach questions ask.
- The four-item list of what a blueprint might involve (theory + evidence, problem + solutions, contrasting perspectives, refuting a belief) is a useful structural vocabulary.
- Three stem categories (Comparative Similarity, Comparative Difference, General Description) cover the question landscape.
- The Overarching Takeaway correctly emphasizes abstract, functional language.
- The importance section makes a smart connection to Structure/Function questions and LR Method of Reasoning.

**Issues:**
- **Comparative-passage bias.** Two of three stem categories are about comparing passages A and B. Single-passage Approach questions (e.g., "The passage can most accurately be described as...") receive only one category. Since many students encounter Approach questions on single passages, this imbalance could mislead their preparation.
- **Redundancy between opening and stem section.** The stem section's intro paragraph restates the question definition.
- **No practice question.**

**Suggested edits:**
- Add a fourth stem category for single-passage Approach questions with more examples.
- Cut the redundant intro paragraph from the stem section.
- Add a mini-exercise asking students to describe a passage's structure in abstract terms.

---

### Lesson 28-2: Step-by-Step Approach (8 blocks)

**Role:** The 2-step method plus two tie-breaker strategies.

**Strengths:**
- The "Passage Blueprint" method (Step 1) is outstanding. The instruction to summarize each paragraph's job using abstract verbs, not content, is the module's best teaching moment.
- The example blueprint (Introduces a theory / Presents challenging evidence / Proposes alternative / Discusses implications) is concrete and well-structured.
- Tie-Breaker #1 ("Focus on the Verbs") is precise: isolating action verbs like "refuting" vs. "qualifying" addresses a real source of student errors.
- Tie-Breaker #2 ("Check the Scope and Emphasis") teaches students to match answer weight to passage weight -- a subtle but important skill.

**Issues:**
- **No worked example.** The blueprint method is described with a sample blueprint but never applied to a full question with answer choices.
- **No question-card.**
- **Step 2 is generic.** "Systematically compare the structural description" could be more specific about what to look for in each answer choice.

**Suggested edits:**
- Add a full worked example showing the blueprint method applied to a sample passage, then matching the blueprint to answer choices.
- Add a question-card.
- In Step 2, provide a checklist: Does the answer match the number of parts? Does it correctly identify what each part does? Does it use accurate verbs?

---

## Structural Issues

### 1. No Practice Questions

The Passage Blueprint method is inherently practical and would benefit enormously from a worked example showing the blueprint → answer-matching process.

**Recommendation:** Add one question-card to Lesson 28-2.

### 2. Comparative-Passage Bias in Stem Categories

The module presents Approach questions as primarily a comparative-passage phenomenon. While comparative passages do feature Approach questions prominently, single-passage versions exist and should receive equal attention.

**Recommendation:** Add a single-passage stem category with examples to Lesson 28-1.
