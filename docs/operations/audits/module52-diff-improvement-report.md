# Module 52: Advanced Passage 2 -- Bentham and Evidence Law -- Website Content Improvement Report

**Scope:** The 7 website lessons (52-1 through 52-7) evaluated as a standalone course unit.
**Source:** PT-109-S-2-P-4 (Questions 23-28)
**Date:** 2026-04-01

---

## Executive Summary

Module 52 is a strong RC Advanced Passage unit built around a legal history passage about Jeremy Bentham's nonexclusion principle. The passage is well-suited for LSAT prep: it presents a historical problem, a proposed reform, criticism of the reform, and its modern legacy. The seven lessons are well-structured and the passage analysis (52-1) is one of the best in the RC sequence. Key improvement opportunities:

1. **Strong passage analysis** -- 52-1 provides excellent paragraph-by-paragraph breakdowns with accessible, conversational language. The "hero of the story" framing in paragraph 3 is particularly engaging.
2. **No "try it yourself" prompts** -- all six question lessons jump directly from the question to the explanation without a pause for independent attempt.
3. **No question-type identification** -- the module covers Main Idea, Author Attitude, Reference/Purpose, Detail/Support, Primary Concern, and Detail questions, but none are labeled or taught as transferable strategies.
4. **Excellent wrong-answer analysis** -- every question lesson includes a full breakdown of all 5 options with clear reasoning. The analyses consistently explain *why* wrong answers are wrong, not just why the right answer is right.
5. **Passage analysis uses engaging language** -- descriptions like "the hero of the story" and "plays devil's advocate" make the material accessible without oversimplifying the legal concepts.

## Lesson-by-Lesson Review

### Lesson 52-1: Passage Analysis (11 blocks)

**Role:** Passage analysis and comprehension guide.

**Strengths:**
- Accordion passage block for full text access
- Five paragraph breakdowns (matching the passage's 5-paragraph structure)
- Conversational, engaging tone: "where we meet the hero of the story" and "plays devil's advocate"
- Each paragraph's function in the argument is clearly identified
- Captures the passage's balanced "reform was good but imperfect" structure accurately

**Issues:**
- **No approach tips for the passage.** A dense legal history passage benefits from guidance on tracking the chronological argument structure (old system -> Bentham's reform -> problems -> modern outcome).
- **No vocabulary support.** Terms like "proscription," "morass," "evidentiary technicalities," and "sacramental confessions" could trip up students unfamiliar with legal terminology.

**Suggested edits:**
- Add a "How to Approach This Passage" callout: "This passage follows a clear timeline. Track: (1) what was wrong before Bentham, (2) what Bentham proposed, (3) the problems with his proposal, (4) how modern law adapted it."
- Add a brief "Key Terms" callout for the most challenging legal vocabulary.

## Structural Issues

### 1. No Active Learning Prompts

All six question lessons (52-2 through 52-7) proceed from question to explanation without any invitation for independent attempt. This is the single most impactful fix across the module.

### 2. No Question-Type Strategy Teaching

The module covers six distinct question types but treats each as an isolated explanation. Adding question-type identification and brief strategy callouts would transform the module from "answer key with commentary" to "transferable RC training."

### 3. Passage Analysis Lacks Approach Guidance

The passage analysis (52-1) does an excellent job breaking down the content but does not teach students how to approach a legal history passage on the LSAT. A brief structural overview would help students tackle similar passages independently.
