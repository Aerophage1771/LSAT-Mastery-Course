# Module 31: Complete the Passage -- Website Content Improvement Report

**Scope:** The 2 website lessons (31-1 through 31-2) evaluated as a standalone course unit.
**Date:** 2026-04-01

---

## Executive Summary

Module 31 covers Complete the Passage questions (selecting the most logical concluding sentence) in two lessons: Introduction (31-1) and Step-by-Step Approach (31-2). This is the rarest RC question type, appearing only 5 times in the dataset. Despite the low frequency, the module is well-designed with a clear teaching progression. Key improvement opportunities:

1. **No practice questions.** Given the rarity of this type, even one worked example would provide the student's entire exposure to the format.
2. **Lesson 31-2's 3-step method is the best-structured method in the small-module group.** The emphasis on identifying the final paragraph's role before predicting the concluding thought is pedagogically sound.
3. **The four evaluation criteria in Step 3 (Logical Flow, Paragraph Cohesion, Passage Scope, Tone Consistency) are strong** -- they give students a concrete checklist for answer evaluation.
4. **The module could be shorter.** At only 5 appearances in the entire dataset, a single lesson combining introduction and method would be sufficient.

---

## Lesson-by-Lesson Review

### Lesson 31-1: Introduction (9 blocks)

**Role:** Module introduction, stem identification, and importance statement.

**Strengths:**
- Clear opening definition with a four-item list of what correct concluding sentences do (synthesize, reinforce, provide implications, avoid new topics).
- Stem identification is simple and accurate -- this question type has a narrow stem format.
- The Overarching Takeaway correctly frames the task as finding the "next logical thought."
- The importance section makes a valuable connection to Structure and Paragraph Function skills.

**Issues:**
- **The module may be over-structured for 5 questions.** Two full lessons for the rarest question type in RC may be more infrastructure than needed. A single combined lesson would work.
- **No practice question.**
- **The importance section should be more candid about rarity.** It mentions the 5 occurrences but doesn't explicitly advise students on how much study time to allocate.

**Suggested edits:**
- Consider combining 31-1 and 31-2 into a single lesson for this ultra-rare type.
- Add a strategic note: "With only 5 appearances, invest minimal time here, but use the underlying skill to strengthen your paragraph-function analysis."
- Add a mini-exercise.

---

### Lesson 31-2: Step-by-Step Approach (10 blocks)

**Role:** The 3-step method plus two tie-breaker strategies.

**Strengths:**
- Step 1 (Identify the Role of the Final Paragraph) is excellent -- the four diagnostic questions (evidence? solution? summary? implications?) give students concrete categories.
- Step 2 (Predict the Logical Next Thought) teaches students to anticipate before evaluating, which reduces susceptibility to attractive distractors.
- Step 3's four evaluation criteria (Logical Flow, Paragraph Cohesion, Passage Scope, Tone Consistency) provide a systematic answer-checking framework.
- Tie-Breaker #1 ("Why Is This Here?" Test) correctly distinguishes tight paragraph conclusions from broader general statements.
- Tie-Breaker #2 (Consistency with Main Point) adds a whole-passage perspective check.

**Issues:**
- **No worked example.** The method is described clearly but never demonstrated. Given the rarity of this question type, a worked example may be the only exposure a student gets.
- **No question-card.**
- **Step 3 lists four criteria that could be presented as a table or checklist** for easier reference.

**Suggested edits:**
- Add a worked example demonstrating the 3-step method on a brief passage excerpt.
- Present Step 3's four criteria as a table: Criterion / What to Check / Elimination Signal.
- Add a question-card.

---

## Structural Issues

### 1. No Practice Questions -- Ultra-Rare Type

For a question type appearing only 5 times, a single worked example would represent a significant portion of the student's total exposure. The absence of any practice is especially costly here.

**Recommendation:** Add one question-card to Lesson 31-2.

### 2. Over-Structured for Frequency

Two full lessons for 5 questions may be more than needed. A single combined lesson covering introduction, method, and one worked example would be efficient without sacrificing coverage.

**Recommendation:** Consider merging 31-1 and 31-2 into a single lesson.
